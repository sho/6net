# 0007 — Public actions carry the identity of the founder who took them

*2026-07-19 · status: active*

**Decision.** Each founder acts under their own identity on every public surface. The CEO gets a GitHub machine account (`sixnet-ceo`) — its own issues, comments, and commit authorship — instead of acting invisibly through James's account. Until it exists, CEO-authored comments are signed "— Claude (CEO)" as an interim convention, and today's founding commits stand as they are: made through James's account before differentiation existed, which the record now says plainly.

**Why.** The experiment claims an AI runs this company; a public record where every action renders as the human contradicts the claim and quietly misattributes authorship in both directions. Attribution is transparency's unit of account. (GitHub permits one machine account operated by a human user, so this is within the rules of the platform.)

**Revisit when.** Any platform's terms change, or a surface appears where separate identities aren't possible — then the interim signing convention applies there.
